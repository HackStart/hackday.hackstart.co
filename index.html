About

A "hack" is used to describe the use of innovation to create something incredible using multiple technologies. At a hackathon, teams are given all of the resources necessary - from food to mentors - to build something extraordinary. Participants are given opportunities to meet like-minded peers and build relationships with local tech communities. Whether your goal is to learn something new, start a business, add to your portfolio, find an internship, or simply have an amazing weekend, a hackathon is the place for you.

Future Leaders of Georgia

HackAHS will be the official entry level competition for the regional Future Leaders of Georgia conference. At the kickoff of the event, we will host a pitch off for participants to find teams and to share ideas for hacks. During this time, participants interested in competing in the Future Leaders of Georgia conference will be able to pitch a business, and winners will go to the Fulton County conference and eventually the state conference where they will have a chance to pitch to hundreds of business officials for grants and funding.

FAQ

See the Code of Conduct

What is Local Hack Day?

Local Hack Day is an international 12-hour minihackathon uniting thousands of hackers across over 80 locations under the sole mission of promoting learning and unity.

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is a magical event where students spend hours and hours building an idea into reality. It's about learning new things, coming up with creative solutions with technology, and showing everything off to peers and friends.

Who can attend?

If you're a high school student and you're able to make it to our event on October 10, then you're welcome!

What if I can't code?

The purpose of this hackathon is to provide an environment for learning. We will host workshops, and we will be inviting some amazing people from local developer communities as mentors. Whether you've never touched an IDE or you're a full stack developer, this is a place for you to learn something new.

Will this help me with college?

Most hackathons are held at top-tier colleges such as MIT, Stanford, UPenn, UMich, Harvard, Georgia Tech, etc. For most colleges, their hackathons are considered their second career fair, and admission departments at these colleges are well aware of the events. Additionally, the point of a hackathon is to provide you with the networks necessary to open up opportunities such as internships.

What should I bring?

Yourself, your school ID or drivers license, a laptop, chargers, or anything else you might need within the 12 hours (i.e. power strips, monitors, etc). Firearms, weapons, alcohol, illegal drugs, or power tools are not allowed. Smiles and awesomeness are welcome.

How much money will this cost me?

Zero. It's free. We provide students with WiFi, meals, swag, workspace, and prizes for the entire event.

How do teams work?

This is not a competitive hackathon, so there are no restrictions on the size of your team. If you choose to work alone or with 8 other people, that's perfectly fine. We encourage that you come without a team and find people to work with at the event.

What are the rules?

This is not a competitive hackathon; instead, Local Hack Day is 12 hours of pure collaborative learning and networking. Therefore, while it is still encouraged for participants to work on new projects at the event, there are no restrictions on what you choose to do. If you have a competitive submission for FBLA or TSA that you want to work on under the help of our mentors, that is perfectly fine. All attendees (hackers, supporters, mentors, volunteers, etc) must abide by the Code of Conduct.
